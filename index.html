A Study of Feathers

White - The Pekin Plumage

Feathers of the stout Pekin, prized for their substance. A duck cloaked in this pure white plumage is one of resilience and strength, known for its hearty and formidable presence in any flock.

Black - The Runner's Shadow

The glossy black of the Runner Duck, a coat built for swiftness. These ducks move like living shadows, quick and unseen, masters of agility who can outpace any threat.

Gold - The Buff's Blessing

Golden feathers of the Buff Duck shimmer with latent magic. In males, this blessing bestows great strength. In females, it promises abundance and life, a truly prosperous plumage.

Grey - The Humble Guise

The simple Grey of the "Ugly Duckling." What many see as plain, the wise know holds the promise of a grand transformation. It is a coat of hidden potential and profound inner strength.

Green - The Mallard's Mark

The familiar green of the Mallard, a color seen across countless ponds. Though called "common," it represents a deep connection to nature and an unparalleled adaptability—the foundation of all duck-kind.
